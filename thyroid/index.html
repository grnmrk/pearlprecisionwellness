The Thyroid Cascade

1

Hypothalamus

TRH

Signals the pituitary to produce TSH based on the body's perceived metabolic need.

2

Pituitary

TSH

Instructs the thyroid to produce T4. The only marker most conventional panels measure.

3

Thyroid Gland

T4

Mostly an inactive storage form. It needs to be converted to active T3 to have any real metabolic effect.

4

Liver & Tissues

Free T3 or RT3

Stress, gut dysfunction, and inflammation shift conversion toward inactive Reverse T3 rather than active Free T3.

5

Every Cell

Free T3 at Receptors

Active T3 drives metabolism, energy, and cognition. Receptor resistance can persist even when T3 levels appear adequate.

A Closer Look

Hashimoto's:
More Than a
Thyroid Problem

Hashimoto's is an immune condition that happens to affect the thyroid. Most people are given a prescription and told their numbers look fine, but the underlying immune process is rarely addressed at all.

We work to find and calm what is driving the immune response, with the goal of lowering antibody levels and giving your thyroid the best chance to function well for the long term.

Trigger Investigation

Gluten sensitivity, intestinal permeability, EBV, H. pylori, and environmental toxin burden

Gut Healing

Restoring intestinal barrier integrity to reduce immune activation driving antibody production

Immune Modulation

Selenium, vitamin D, omega-3s, and adaptogenic botanicals with evidence for antibody reduction

Dietary Strategy

Eliminating dietary triggers and supporting anti-inflammatory pathways that calm immune reactivity

Stress Physiology

Chronic stress can both trigger and perpetuate autoimmune flares. HPA axis burden is always part of our assessment.

Hormone Optimization

When replacement is needed, optimizing type, dose, and timing based on labs and symptoms together

Discuss Your Testing

TSH

Interpreted against an optimal range of ~1–2 mIU/L, correlated with symptoms

Free T4 & Free T3

Unbound, bioavailable forms of both hormones: T4 for production and Free T3 for the active form at receptor sites

Reverse T3

Identifying disproportionate conversion to the inactive blocking form, which is commonly missed by standard panels

TPO & Thyroglobulin Antibodies

Screening for Hashimoto's, which is often detectable years before TSH becomes abnormal

Nutritional Cofactors

Selenium, iodine, zinc, ferritin, and vitamin D, all essential for hormone production and conversion

Adrenal & Cortisol Panel

DUTCH panel evaluating HPA axis function, which directly modulates thyroid conversion
