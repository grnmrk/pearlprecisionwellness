Understanding the problem

Why Hormones
Get Out of
Balance

Hormones don't exist in isolation. Estrogen, progesterone, cortisol, thyroid hormones, insulin, and androgens are all part of a deeply interconnected system — and when one is dysregulated, others follow.

Conventional labs often test for the most obvious imbalances, but miss the subtler patterns that drive real symptoms: estrogen dominance in the presence of technically "normal" levels, cortisol dysregulation that doesn't show up as frank adrenal failure, or thyroid function that falls within range but is nowhere near optimal.

Functional medicine reinterprets these signals differently. We look at the full hormonal cascade, the upstream triggers, and the downstream effects — not just whether a number falls inside a reference range.

The gap in conventional care

Why "Your Labs Look Fine" Isn't the Full Picture

Conventional hormone panels typically test TSH, estradiol, and sometimes testosterone — evaluated against broad population reference ranges designed to catch disease, not optimize health.

Functional medicine uses optimal ranges, not just normal ranges. It looks at ratios between hormones, the rhythm of cortisol throughout the day, how hormones are being metabolized, and what's driving the pattern in the first place.

"The goal isn't just to push a number into range. It's to understand why it's off — and what that's telling us about your physiology."
